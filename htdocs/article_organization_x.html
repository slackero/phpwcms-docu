The organization of articles in structure levels
If a visitor (e.g. via navigation) arrives at a structure level, one of the following is shown
to the visitor of the Website, depending upon number of articles contained in that
structure level:

If the level contains no article, the default error message from the template is
presented: "An error occurred. Please contact the Webmaster."

If the level does contain an article, the full article is shown.

If the level contains more than one article, a menu is shown which lists the articles
contained in the structure level in descending chronological order. The most current
article is represented in the list with title and summary, while all other articles only with
their title. By clicking, the visitor can select which article he would like to read (this
works well on a news page, for example).

You can shift the position of an article within its structure level by clicking on its
symbols.

If you want to shift an article into another structure level, proceed as follows:

1. Click on the symbol  corresponding to the article to be shifted. The symbol changes to , in order to indicate that it is selected.


2. Click on the symbol  of the structure level in which you would like to move the article into.
